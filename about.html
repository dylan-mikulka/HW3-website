My Fly-Fishing Journey

How It All Began

I've been fishing for as long as I can remember. My family jokes that I had a rod in my hand before I could even walk properly. But my real journey with fly-fishing began when I was nine years old. My grandfather took me out to a small stream and taught me how to cast. I still remember the feel of the rod, the rhythm of the line in the air, and the excitement of my first trout on a fly. From that moment on, I was hooked.

Every summer growing up, I would go on weekly fishing trips with my grandparents. We'd load up the old Oldtown canoe and paddle out into the rivers and lakes of Maine, searching for smallmouth bass. Those long days on the water, with nothing but the sound of paddles dipping and the occasional splash of a fish, are some of my most cherished memories. My grandparents taught me more than just how to fish. They taught me patience, respect for nature, and the value of simply being outdoors.

These days, my focus has shifted to wading the rivers of northern Maine with my father. We target brook trout and landlocked salmon in cold, clear streams that wind through dense forests. But it's never just about the fishing. Every trip is an opportunity to witness incredible wildlife. I've watched moose feeding along the banks, bald eagles soaring overhead, and beavers working on their dams. These encounters remind me why I love this sport so much. It's about being part of something larger.

The last two summers, my dad and I took on a different kind of project. We spent our time off the grid in northern Maine, building a cabin from the ground up. Working with our hands, sleeping under the stars, and living without modern conveniences brought me even closer to the wild places I love. That cabin now serves as our base camp for fishing adventures, a place where we can disconnect from everything and just be in nature.

Essential Fly-Fishing Gear

  • Fly Rod: 9-foot, 5-weight rod for all-around versatility
  • Fly Reel: Quality reel with smooth drag system
  • Fly Line: Weight-forward floating line for most conditions
  • Leaders & Tippet: 9-foot tapered leaders, various tippet sizes
  • Flies: Selection of dry flies, nymphs, and streamers
  • Waders & Boots: Breathable waders with felt or rubber soles
  • Vest or Pack: To carry all your gear and accessories
  • Polarized Sunglasses: Essential for seeing into the water
